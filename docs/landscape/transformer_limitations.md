# Transformer Limitations

Transformer architectures have driven much of the modern AI revolution.

They have enabled major advances across:

- language modeling
- coding systems
- multimodal systems
- reasoning benchmarks
- generative AI

At the same time, transformers continue to face important limitations and scaling pressures.

## Publicly Discussed Challenges

Examples of publicly discussed challenges include:

- quadratic attention scaling
- memory cost
- long-context degradation
- unstable long-horizon reasoning
- hallucinations
- retrieval fragmentation
- high inference cost
- scaling inefficiency

## Why the Industry Is Exploring Alternatives

These pressures have contributed to growing interest in:

- state space models
- recurrent systems
- sparse architectures
- retrieval systems
- memory-augmented systems
- hybrid architectures
- efficient attention systems

## Important Tradeoffs

Transformers also provide major strengths:

- broad capability scaling
- flexible representation learning
- strong ecosystem support
- highly optimized tooling

Alternative systems may improve some properties while weakening others.

## Why This Matters to CORE

CORE is interested in architectural directions that may improve:

- reliability
- efficiency
- retention
- transfer
- long-horizon behavior
- repeatability

This repository intentionally avoids disclosing private architectural mechanisms.
