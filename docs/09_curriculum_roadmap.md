# CORE Curriculum Roadmap

Disclosure label: `PUBLIC_SUMMARY_ONLY`

This document provides a high-level public description of CORE's educational and evaluation direction. It intentionally avoids implementation-level detail, curriculum-generation logic, internal gating systems, proprietary corpus design, verifier architecture, and patent-sensitive mechanisms.

## Public Educational Direction

CORE privately explores whether stronger foundational reasoning and staged evaluation can improve reliability, transfer, and long-term learning behavior.

Publicly discussable themes include:

- foundational reasoning,
- structured learning progression,
- careful evaluation discipline,
- cross-domain transfer,
- multilingual readiness exploration,
- emphasis on verification and limitations.

Public materials should avoid describing:

- internal curriculum sequencing logic,
- internal gate structure,
- verifier implementation,
- weighting systems,
- representation-space assumptions,
- private corpus composition,
- internal learning-state assumptions,
- implementation-specific educational architecture.

## Illustrative Public Learning Areas

Examples of broad learning categories under private exploration may include:

- language and communication,
- reasoning and logic,
- mathematics,
- scientific literacy,
- history and source analysis,
- philosophy and ethics,
- software engineering and systems thinking.

These categories are illustrative only and should not be interpreted as a disclosure of the private curriculum architecture, training pipeline, or learning-order implementation.

## Evaluation Philosophy

CORE's public-facing evaluation posture emphasizes:

- evidence over hype,
- explicit limitations,
- controlled evaluation conditions,
- distinction between readiness and mastery,
- separation between public summaries and private implementation.

## Disclosure Reminder

This roadmap describes high-level educational direction only. Internal curriculum generators, evaluation systems, corpus-selection methods, validation logic, and related implementation details remain private pending legal and IP review.
