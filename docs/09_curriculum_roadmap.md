# CORE Curriculum Roadmap

Disclosure label: `PUBLIC_SUMMARY_ONLY`

The CORE engine follows an authoritative, multi-tier curriculum execution sequence. This roadmap outlines the architectural progression of knowledge acquisition, from foundational linguistic structures to advanced specialty domains.

## The Learning Architecture: A Tiered Discipline

CORE does not ingest data indiscriminately. It follows a four-tier sequence where no tier may begin until every "Mastery Gate" in the prior tier is verified.

### Tier 0: Language Foundation (Primary Family)
The primary language family (comprising modern and ancient high-morphology variants) forms the triangulated coordinate system for the model's meaning-space.
- **Milestone**: Trilingual geometric alignment pass.
- **Focus**: Semantic anchoring, morphological stability, and cross-linguistic isomorphism.

---

### Tier 1: Core Knowledge (101 Series)
Introductory core knowledge, focused on the **Foundations of Reasoning**.

1. **Epistemology 101 (REQUIRED FIRST)**: How knowledge works, how it fails, and how to verify it independently.
2. **Logic 101**: Formal and informal reasoning, propositional logic, and fallacy recognition.
3. **Mathematics 101**: The mathematical concept of number, integers, and basic proof structure.
4. **Natural Science 101**: The structure of empirical reasoning and the scientific method.
5. **History 101**: Chronological causation and primary vs. secondary source analysis.

---

### Tier 2: Core Knowledge (102 Series)
Completion of general knowledge foundations.

- **Epistemology 102**: Institutional sociology of knowledge and verification chain construction.
- **Logic 102**: Modal logic, set theory, and formal proof calculus.
- **Mathematics 102**: Calculus, statistics, and linear algebra foundations.
- **Natural Science 102**: In-depth physical models and anomalous data evaluation.
- **History 102**: Geopolitics, rhetoric, and narrative bias analysis.

---

### Tier 3: Specialty Domains
Advanced domains that sit on the completed foundation of Tiers 1 and 2.
- **Sacred Texts Hermeneutics**: High-fidelity interpretation using the trilingual foundation.
- **Philosophy**: Metaphysics, ontology, and formal ethics.
- **Law**: Legal reasoning and statutory interpretation.
- **Medicine**: Pathological reasoning and institutional system analysis.
- **Software Engineering**: System architecture and computational complexity.

---

## Key Epistemic Features

### Epistemology-First Learning
Unlike standard models that "learn everything at once," CORE learns **how to evaluate information** (Epistemology) before it is permitted to ingest domain knowledge. This governs the model's epistemic posture throughout its lifecycle.

### Source Trust Weighting
Every piece of information ingested carries an explicit **Source Trust Weight**. The model learns to distinguish between primary sources, peer-reviewed consensus, textbooks, and institutional authority, treating the latter as a human product subject to incentive structures and bias.

### The Ethics Thread
Ethics is not a standalone course but a thread that advances through the curriculum:
- **Personal**: Intellectual integrity and self-deception (Tier 1).
- **Institutional**: How organizations corrupt knowledge (Tier 2).
- **Systemic**: How entire civilizations operate on manufactured narratives (Tier 2/3).

---

## Verification Discipline

Mastery is not optional. Every stage of every tier must pass a **Mastery Verification Gate** (Milestone verification). A course is not considered "learned" simply because the data was processed; the engine's internal manifold must prove it has acquired the structure.
