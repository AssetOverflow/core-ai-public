# Structured Mastery

CORE is interested in whether AI systems can move beyond shallow pattern completion toward more reusable and durable forms of learned structure.

This repository refers to that direction broadly as structured mastery.

## Why It Matters

A system can appear impressive on isolated tasks while still struggling with:

- retention
- transfer
- long-horizon coherence
- compositional reasoning
- stability
- reusable structure

Shallow success does not necessarily imply durable understanding.

## The Central Question

Can AI systems develop forms of learned structure that:

- persist more reliably
- transfer across related problems
- remain reusable over time
- support long-horizon reasoning
- reduce brittle behavior

CORE is interested in this question from both an architectural and evaluation perspective.

## Public Themes

Public themes associated with structured mastery include:

- retention
- transfer
- compositional reasoning
- reusable structure
- repeatability
- benchmark progression
- long-horizon reliability

## Evaluation Perspective

CORE is interested in evaluations that measure:

- progression over time
- transfer to related tasks
- retention after delay or boundary
- repeatability under repeated evaluation
- resistance to hallucination-like behavior

## Important Boundary

This repository intentionally avoids disclosing private implementation details related to how CORE attempts to pursue these goals.
