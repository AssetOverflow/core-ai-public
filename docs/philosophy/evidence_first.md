# Evidence-First AI Development

CORE is being developed with an evidence-first philosophy.

The goal is not merely to produce impressive demonstrations. The goal is to produce systems whose behavior can be measured, evaluated, reproduced, and improved with disciplined engineering practices.

## Why This Matters

Modern AI development can sometimes drift toward:

- benchmark theater
- selective demonstrations
- unclear evaluation methodology
- hidden failure modes
- poorly reproducible claims
- hype-driven positioning

This creates confusion about what systems can actually do reliably.

## CORE Philosophy

CORE prioritizes:

- measurable progress
- evaluation discipline
- benchmark integrity
- reproducibility practices
- negative controls
- transparent limitations
- engineering rigor
- careful public claims

## Public Evidence Philosophy

Public-facing evidence should:

- be understandable
- be measurable
- avoid misleading framing
- include limitations
- avoid implementation leakage
- preserve benchmark integrity

## Benchmark Integrity

Benchmarks should measure genuine capability rather than memorization, leakage, or optimization against the benchmark itself.

This repository intentionally withholds private benchmark generators and implementation details to preserve evaluation integrity.

## Public Communication Standard

CORE should be communicated through:

- disciplined excitement
- truthful claims
- measurable evidence
- transparent limitations
- rigorous engineering culture

The goal is long-term credibility, not short-term hype.
